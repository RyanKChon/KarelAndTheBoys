  Answer here What does PID stand for? What is the purpose of the P, the I, and the D? What steps did you take to choose the best values for your robot? Explain this like you would for someone taking this course next year.

  PID stands for proportional control, integral control, and derivative control. the proportional control takes the current error value and scales it in relation to your porportional gain in order to get the desired turn rate. Essentially what this means is that if you robot is veering off to the right, it will allow your motors to turn at a certain speed, in this instance the left slows down and the right speeds up, so that it realigns with the line that it was following. I is the integral control. The purpose of integral control is to take the sum of your past errors. After being multiplied by a greater error sum which will deal with small errors and inconsistencies. D is the derivitive control, which predicts and increase or decrease by taking the current error value and subtracting it from the last error value so that the turn rate will be changed beforehand, helping with sharp turns.
